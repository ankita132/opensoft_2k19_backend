Punjab Copying Fees Act, 1936_Section Preamble--> State(s): Haryana An Act to facilitate the recovery of fees payable for copies made or supplied of records in offices under the control of Revenue, Judicial and other officers of Government. Whereas it is expedient to facilitate the recovery of fees payable for copies made or supplied of records kept in offices under the control of Revenue, Judicial and other officers of Government in the [Haryana]1 and whereas the previous sanction of the Governor-General, required under subsection (3) of section 80-A of the Government of India Act, has been obtained; it is hereby enacted as follows:- Substituted by Haryana Adaptation of Laws (State and Concurrent Subjects) Order, 1968
Punjab Copying Fees Act, 1936_Section 1--> State(s): Haryana (1) This Act may be called the Punjab Copying Fees Act, 1936.1 (2) It extends to [Haryana]2 . (3) It shall come into force on such date 3 as the 4 [State] Government may be notification appoint in this behalf. Received the assent of his Excellency the Governor on the 4th November, 1936 and that of his Excellency the Viceroy and Governor General on the 24th November, 1936, and was first published in the Punjab Gazette Extraordinary on the 27th November, 1936 Substituted by Haryana Adaptation of Laws (State and Concurrent Subjects) Order, 1968 This Act came into force on 2nd January, 1937, vide Punjab Government notification No. 6133-A, dated 19th December, 1936 Substituted by Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950, for "Provincial."
Punjab Copying Fees Act, 1936_Section 2--> State(s): Haryana In this Act "record" includes any portion of a record and any document, plan, map or other paper attached thereto or forming part of the record of any suit or appeal, enquiry or trial or other proceeding in any court or office.
Punjab Copying Fees Act, 1936_Section 3--> State(s): Haryana When any copy of any record has been made at the request of any applicant or his agent and such applicant or his agent has refused to accept delivery of the same or when any copy has been supplied to any such applicant or his agent, and the fee or any portion of the fee leviable for the supply of such copy remains unpaid the said fee or portion thereof may be recovered from the applicant as if it were an arrears of land revenue: Provided that a pleader presenting such an application on behalf of a client will not be held personally responsible where the application bears the signature or thumb-impression of such client.
