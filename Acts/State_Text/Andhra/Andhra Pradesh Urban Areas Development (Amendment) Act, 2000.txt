Andhra Pradesh Urban Areas Development (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section Preamble--> State(s): Andhra Pradesh An Act Further to Amend the Andhra Pradesh Urban Areas (Development) Act, 1975. Be it enacted by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Andhra Pradesh in the Fifty-first Year of the Republic of India as follows:-
Andhra Pradesh Urban Areas Development (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 1--> State(s): Andhra Pradesh (1) This Act may be called the Andhra Pradesh Urban Areas (Development) (Amendment) Act, 2000.1 (2) It shall be deemed to have come into force on and from the 19th February, 1998. Received the assent of the Governor on the 27th April, 2000 and the said assent is hereby first published on the 28th April, 2000
Andhra Pradesh Urban Areas Development (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 2--> State(s): Andhra Pradesh In the Andhra Pradesh Urban Areas (Development) Act, 1975, in section 33, in sub-section (1), in the proviso for the words "in the aggregate exceed six years" the words in the aggregate exceed two years", shall be substituted.
Andhra Pradesh Urban Areas Development (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 3--> State(s): Andhra Pradesh Notwithstanding anything contained in the principal Act, the continuation of the Special Officer, Puttaparthi Urban Development Authority appointed in G.O.Ms. No. 109, Municipal Administration Department, dated the 10th February, 1992 during the period from 29th February, 1998 to the 4th April, 1998 shall not be deemed to be invalid or ever to have become invalid and accordingly,- (i) anything done or any action taken by such Special officer during the said period shall be deemed to have been validly done or taken as if such Special Officer has not ceased to hold office; and (ii) the Special Officer appointed in the said Government Order shall continue to hold office with effect from the 19th February, 1998 as if his appointment has been extended upto 4th April, 1998, the date on which the Sri Satya Sai Urban Development Authority was constituted in G.O.Ms.No.139, M.A. & U.D. Department, dated 4-4-1998 in supersession of the orders issued in G.O.Ms. No. 109, H.A., dated 19-2-1992. (G. Bhavani Prasad), Secretary to Government, Legislative Affairs & Justice, Law Department.
