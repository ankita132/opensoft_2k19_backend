Gujarat Fiscal Responsibility (Amendment) Act, 2011_Section Preamble--> State(s): Gujarat An Act further to amend the Gujarat Fiscal Responsibility Act, 2005. It is hereby enacted in the Sixty-second Year of the Republic of India as follows:-
Gujarat Fiscal Responsibility (Amendment) Act, 2011_Section 1--> State(s): Gujarat This Act may be called the Gujarat Fiscal Responsibility (Amendment) Act, 2011.
Gujarat Fiscal Responsibility (Amendment) Act, 2011_Section 2--> State(s): Gujarat In the Gujarat Fiscal Responsibility Act, 2005, (Guj. 11 of 2005), for Section 5, the following section shall be substituted, namely:- "5. Fiscal managements targets:- In particular, and without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing provisions, the State Government shall- (a) reduce the revenue deficit to zero by the financial year, commencing from the 1.04.2011 and ending on 31.03.2012 and maintain at that level or generate revenue surplus thereafter; (b) reduce fiscal deficit to not more than three per cent, of the estimated Gross State Domestic Product of the year, from the financial year commencing from 1.04.2011 and ending on 31.03.2012 and maintain at that level thereafter; (c) cap the total public debt of the State Government so as not to exceed 27.1 per cent, of the estimated Gross State Domestic Product of the year, for each of the financial years commencing from 1.01.2011 and ending on 31.03.2015; (d) cap outstanding guarantees within the limit provided in the Gujarat State Guarantees Act, 1963 (Guj. XXII of 1963): Provided that revenue deficit and fiscal deficit may exceed the limits specified under this section due to ground or grounds of unforeseen demands on the finances of the State Government arising out of internal disturbance or natural calamity or due to any other exceptional ground as the State Government may specify: Provided further that a statement in respect of the ground or grounds specified in the first proviso shall be placed before the State Legislature, as soon as may be, after such deficit amount exceeds the aforesaid targets.".
